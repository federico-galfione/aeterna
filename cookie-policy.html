Cookie Policy

Last updated: February 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how Aeterna Events ("we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit our website at aeternaevents.com. This policy should be read alongside our Privacy Policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences and improve your browsing experience. Cookies can be "session" cookies (which are deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent" cookies (which remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them).

2. Cookies We Use

Our website uses a minimal number of cookies. We do not use analytics cookies, advertising cookies, or tracking pixels. Below is a detailed list of the cookies used on our website:

Cookie Name Type Purpose Duration
aeterna_cookie_consent First-party, Necessary Stores your cookie consent preference so the banner doesn't appear on every visit Persistent (until cleared)

3. Third-Party Cookies

Our website loads fonts from Google Fonts. When you visit our site, your browser makes requests to Google's servers to load these fonts. Google may set cookies or collect information such as:

  • Your IP address
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • The page you visited

For detailed information about how Google processes this data, please see Google's Privacy Policy.

4. Cookies We Do NOT Use

To protect your privacy, we have chosen not to use:

  • Analytics cookies: We do not use Google Analytics or similar tracking tools.
  • Advertising cookies: We do not display advertisements or use retargeting.
  • Social media cookies: We do not embed social media plugins that track your activity.
  • Marketing pixels: We do not use Facebook Pixel, LinkedIn Insight Tag, or similar tracking technologies.

5. Local Storage

In addition to cookies, we use your browser's local storage to remember your cookie consent preference. Local storage is similar to cookies but allows for larger amounts of data to be stored. The data stored in local storage:

  • Is only accessible by our website
  • Is not sent to our servers
  • Remains until you clear your browser data

6. Managing Cookies

You have several options to manage cookies:

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically:

  • View cookies stored on your device
  • Delete all or specific cookies
  • Block cookies from all or specific websites
  • Set your browser to notify you when a cookie is set

Here are links to cookie management instructions for popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Clearing Local Storage

To clear your cookie consent preference, you can clear your browser's local storage. This is typically found in the same settings area as cookie management.

Impact of Disabling Cookies

Since our website uses very few cookies, disabling cookies should not significantly affect your experience. However, the cookie consent banner will appear on every visit if cookies are disabled.

7. Legal Basis

Under the EU ePrivacy Directive and GDPR:

  • Strictly necessary cookies (like our consent cookie) do not require consent as they are essential for the website to function.
  • Non-essential cookies require your consent before being set. We do not use non-essential cookies.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
